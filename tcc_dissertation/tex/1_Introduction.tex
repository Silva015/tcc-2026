\label{chap:introduction}

A great Introduction chapter is divided into five base parts: contextualization, problem description, research objevtives, contributions, and the presentation of the organization of the whole document.


\section{Problem Statement}
\label{sec_problem}

Explain in this section what is your work about. It is a concise, structured explanation of the specific issue, gap in knowledge, or "pain point" that your work intends to address. Think of it as the "Why" behind your TCC. It proves that your research isn't just a random exercise, but a necessary response to a real-world or theoretical challenge.

A strong problem description typically follows a logical flow from what should be happening to what is actually happening:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The Ideal: the desired state or the goal (e.g., "AI models should be both accurate and computationally efficient").
    \item The Reality: describes the current situation or the barrier preventing the ideal state (e.g., "Current deep learning models require massive datasets and high power").
    \item The Gap: describes what is missing or failing (e.g., "Existing architectures struggle with small-sample medical datasets").
    \item The Consequence: explain why this matters. What happens if we don't solve this? (e.g., "This leads to delayed diagnostics in emergency settings").
\end{enumerate}


\section{Research Objectives}
\label{sec_goals}

This section complements the previous one. This section first to determine the scope of your work. It is a roadmap for your study. While the Problem Statement section explains what is wrong, the Research Objectives section state exactly what you plan to accomplish to solve the problem. Here you must to translate your broad research goals into actionable, measurable steps.

Example: "To develop an optimized classification system for ballistic wounds using Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN) and Transfer Learning." (general objective).

You can complete the general objective with specific (sub) objectives:

- To identify the primary visual features of different types of projectile trauma through a review of forensic literature.
- To implement a Transfer Learning framework for feature extraction from a specialized medical image dataset.
- To design a KAN-based architecture to classify wound types with high accuracy.
- To compare the computational performance of the KAN model against traditional architectures.

NOTICE: USE A DISSERTATIVE STYLE. DO NOT USE ITEMIZATIONS NOR LLM-STYLE SENTENCES.


\subsection{Research Hypothesis}
\label{sec_hypothesis}

A research hypothesis is essentially an "guess" or a tentative prediction about the outcome of your study. It is a declarative statement that you intend to test through your research. If the Research Objective is what you want to do, the Hypothesis is what you expect to find.

\section{Contributions}
\label{sec_contributions}

Section dedicated to make more explicit the contributions of your study. Included in the final of the Introduction chapter, it is is a explicit statement of the specific value, new knowledge, or technical advancement your work provides to the scientific community.

While the Objectives say what you did, the Contributions say why the field is better off now that you've done it. The Contribution is about originality of your work.
