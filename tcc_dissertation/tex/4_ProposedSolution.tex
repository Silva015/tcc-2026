Including a "Proposed Solution" chapter as a standalone chapter is actually a very sophisticated practice in Engineering and Computer Science theses.

It acts as a bridge between the "What is out there" (Related Works) and the "How I did it" (Methodology).

Since you are using KAN Networks (a relatively new architecture) combined with Transfer Learning, a separate chapter allows you to explain your "innovation" without getting bogged down in the boring details of data collection or software versions.

The idea is to make a special chapter to make your contribution evident.
