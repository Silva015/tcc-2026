\label{chap:methodology}

The methodology chapter serves as the technical backbone of your research, providing a transparent and replicable account of how you conducted your study. 

Its goal is to establish procedural validity. It convinces the reader that your results are not accidental but the product of a rigorous, systematic application of scientific methods. 

This chapter transforms the "blueprint" from your proposed solution into a concrete "construction rtecipe" that another researcher could follow to achieve the exact same outcomes.

It starts with the experimental design, where you define the scope and the controlled variables of your tests. You must describe the materials and dataset in detaisl, explaining the origin of the ballistic wound images, the preprocessing steps taken (such as normalization or data augmentationtechniques) and the justification for each choices.

It leads into the implementation details, where the abstract KAN architecture is mapped onto physical reality. Here, you discuss the software environment, specific libraries, and the hardware used, effectively bridging the gap between theory and execution.


Unlike the previous "Proposed Solution" chapter, which is theoretical and architectural, the methodology chapter is descriptive and objective. It should be written in a way that prioritizes clarity and precision. Every technical choice made during the experiment (such as the number of epochs or the choice of a specific spline degree in the KAN layers) must be documented here so that the reader can trust the integrity of the data that follows.
